What is E26?

E26 is the unified requirement (UR E26) for cyber resilience introduced by IACS and adopted by major classification societies. It ensures that new ships contracted after January 2024 are designed, built, and delivered with robust cybersecurity measures.

Read the full official regulation here (IACS UR E26 Cyber Resilience).

Why It Matters

  • Protects critical OT systems like navigation, propulsion, and cargo management.
  • Ensures compliance with IMO MSC-FAL.1/Circ.3 and IACS UR E26.
  • Reduces risk of cyber incidents, safeguarding crew, environment, and reputation.

Stages of Compliance

  1. Risk Assessment: Identify and prioritize cyber threats to OT and IT systems.
  2. Design Protection: Architect system segregation, firewalls, and secure access into ship design.
  3. Build & Verification: Implement controls during construction, conduct vulnerability scans and tests.
  4. Delivery & Certification: Submit evidence and obtain E26 compliance certification from class society.
